The gap between the 2026 Vision and what has shipped

Miami-Dade's 2026 AI Vision names seven roadmap priorities. The "No Wrong Door" 311 experience, the WISE305 permitting chatbot, and the countywide Copilot Chat rollout through InnovateUS and Brainstorm are the three that determine whether 2026 ships a headline or a footnote. The AI Advisory Council's seven workgroups have the governance shape in place. The gap is the orchestration layer that sits above 26 department systems and decides what context each resident interaction actually needs. This page is a short read on that gap, and on where Eduba can help.

The working frame

The working rule of thumb for a county stack of this shape: roughly 60 percent of the work is traditional code and data access across the existing lines of business, about 30 percent is rule-based logic (eligibility, escalation, routing, compliance gates), and about 10 percent is genuine AI work. Most of the "No Wrong Door" request volume does not need a large language model. It needs the right context retrieved from the right department and handed to the right place, with a model used only where fluency and ambiguity justify it. Eduba's work is deciding, per workflow, which layer each step belongs on. Microsoft Copilot Chat stays where it is. The orchestration layer sits on top of it.

A relevant precedent

Eduba's closest precedent is an enterprise Copilot and AI-enablement program delivered across Pacific Life and Colgate-Palmolive through Correlation One. 1,500+ people trained since May 2025. 6,000 to 9,000 hours saved per year. 95% of participants still using the tools 30 days after the workshop. The mechanism that carried those numbers was workflow capture during the workshop and a structured artifact set that each cohort member walked away with. That mechanism is portable to the county's InnovateUS and Miami Dade College training layer without displacing it.

The compliance-shaped use case

For the WISE305 permitting chatbot and the 311 cross-department agent, a compliance-heavy precedent applies. Eduba's VigilOre engagement compressed 160+ hours of regulatory review work to under five minutes using a folder-structured context architecture. The same structure is what makes escalation paths explicit and defensible, which is the requirement for any AI surface that residents interact with directly.

The methodology of record

Interpretable Context Methodology (ICM), published in ACM Transactions on Interactive Intelligent Systems. The core claim is that agent context, organized as a layered filesystem, produces measurable interpretability and reproducibility gains. For a Board-accountable county AI program, interpretability is the property that matters. Repo: github.com/RinDig/Interpretable-Context-Methodology-ICM-.
